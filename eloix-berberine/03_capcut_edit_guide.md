# ELOIX Berberine 4-in-1 — CapCut Edit Guide (VO + B-roll, first project)

A step-by-step CapCut build for the 6-scene faceless ad. Total target: **~35s, 9:16, 1080x1920.**
Golden rule for VO + B-roll: **the voiceover is the timeline. Everything cuts to it.**

---

## 0) Project setup

1. New Project → set **Aspect Ratio 9:16**.
2. Settings → **Resolution 1080p, Frame rate 30fps**.
3. Turn on the **safe-area / title-safe guides** if available. Keep all text out of the **top ~15%** and **bottom ~20%** (that's where TikTok/Reels/Shorts UI sits).

## 1) Lay the backbone (voiceover first)

1. Import all 6 voiceover lines (one clip per scene, or one continuous VO track).
2. Drop the VO on the timeline first. **Do not add visuals yet.**
3. Add markers where each line starts. This becomes your cut grid.

## 2) Add the visuals to each line

Import your 6 AI/stock clips. Place each clip so it **starts a hair before its VO line** (about 3-5 frames of lead) and trim it to line length + a short breath. Follow the storyboard's cut triggers.

---

## Scene-by-scene edit + pacing

| Scene | Line (VO) | Clip length | Cut trigger / note |
|------|-----------|-------------|--------------------|
| 1 Hook | "If you eat clean and still crash..." | ~4s | Fastest scene. Visual must be on-screen on the very first word. |
| 2 Found it | "I don't usually trust supplements..." | ~7s | Bottle comes into focus on "looked into it." |
| 3 Results | "Within a couple weeks, my energy..." | ~7s | Same desk as Sc1, opposite energy (match-cut feel). |
| 4 Surprise | "My favorite part? ...four clean ingredients" | ~7s | Quick 0.5-1s pantry flash on "cravings faded," then capsules on "four clean ingredients." |
| 5 CTA setup | "Right now it's 30% off..." | ~6s | "30% OFF" text pops on the spoken "30% off." |
| 6 CTA close | "Try it for 30 days..." | ~5s | Hold on centered bottle. FDA disclaimer on screen the whole time. |

**Pacing feel:** punchy open (Sc1 fast), then settle into a calm, honest middle (Sc2-4), then an easy low-pressure close (Sc5-6). Never let a clip sit longer than its line — trim dead air aggressively. Keep total ~35s.

## Transitions (keep it real, not cinematic)

- **Default = hard cut** (no transition) between every scene. This is the authentic UGC look and it converts.
- Only exception: a **0.1-0.2s "Dip to Black"** is acceptable when the *location* changes (desk → kitchen, i.e. Sc3→Sc4). Optional.
- **Avoid** CapCut's flashy transitions (glitch, zoom blur, spin, 3D, prism). They scream "ad" and fight realism.
- For Sc1→Sc3 you can use a **match cut** (same desk framing) — just hard-cut; the matching composition does the work.

## SFX (subtle — one ambience per scene + tiny foley)

In CapCut: **Audio → Sound effects**. Search and place low, quiet hits.

- **Ambience beds (very low, ~-24 dB):** "office room tone" / "quiet room" for Sc1-3; "kitchen ambience" for Sc4-6.
- **Foley, synced to action:**
  - Sc1: soft "finger tap on desk."
  - Sc3: gentle "glass / water set down."
  - Sc4: light "pill bottle shake" or "capsule rattle."
  - Sc5: soft "bottle set on counter" clink.
- **UI:** one gentle "pop" / "notification soft" when the **30% OFF** text appears (Sc5). Keep it soft, not a hard ding.
- Optional: a very subtle "whoosh" into Sc2 — but dry hard cuts are totally fine. Less is more.

## BGM

1. **Audio → Music.** Pick a warm, mellow, low-key track (lo-fi, soft acoustic, gentle "morning" underscore). Nothing dramatic/cinematic.
2. **Level it under the voice:** set BGM to roughly **-20 to -24 dB** so the VO always wins.
3. **Duck it under the VO:** CapCut mobile doesn't always auto-duck reliably, so do it manually — select the music clip, add **volume keyframes**, and dip it ~4-6 dB lower during each spoken line, back up in the gaps.
4. **Licensing (important — this is a PAID ad):** CapCut's in-app music and trending TikTok sounds are generally **not cleared for paid ads**. For a real ad, use **TikTok's Commercial Music Library**, Meta's Sound Collection, or a royalty-free license (Epidemic Sound, Artlist, Uppbeat). Swap the temp track before you publish.

## Captions / subtitle template

1. **Text → Auto captions** → generate from the VO track. Then fix any misread words by hand.
2. **Template settings (UGC style that converts):**
   - **Font:** heavy/bold clean sans (e.g., a bold default like "Aa Bold"). Avoid thin or decorative fonts.
   - **Size:** big — aim for ~2-4 words visible per line.
   - **Position:** centered horizontally, placed around **60-65% down the frame** — above the bottom-20% UI zone, below the middle. Never at the very bottom.
   - **Style:** white text with a **black outline or soft drop shadow** (or a subtle semi-transparent background bar) so it reads on any footage.
   - **Animation:** subtle **word-by-word / karaoke highlight** (CapCut "Highlight" caption animation) — energetic but not gimmicky. Keep it consistent across all scenes.
   - **Keyword pop:** color-highlight the money words — "**crash**," "**cravings**," "**30% off**," "**money back**" — in a single accent color (a soft green ties to the ELOIX label).

3. **On-screen text overlays (separate from captions):**
   - **Sc5 "30% OFF":** bold small tag, lower-third or upper-corner, **appears on the spoken "30% off."** Plain text — never a fake button/UI.
   - **Sc6 FDA disclaimer:** small but legible, lower-middle safe area, on screen for the full 5s hold:
     > "These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."
   - Optional **Sc6 "30-day money-back"** clean text tag alongside the bottle.

- [ ] Captions inside the safe area, readable on footage, keywords highlighted.
